# Electronic structure and reactivity analyses

## Frontiers and orbitals

- HOMO/LUMO/SOMO pictures show orbital spatial form and phase.
- The two orbital colors mean `+ψ` and `-ψ`, not positive/negative charge.
- Kohn-Sham HOMO-LUMO gap is not automatically the optical or fundamental gap.
- Compare molecules only at the same method and orbital isovalue.

## Charges and bond indices

Mulliken, Löwdin, Hirshfeld, CM5, NPA/NBO, CHELPG/MK and related charges are
method definitions, not observables. Compare trends using one scheme and, for
important claims, at least one complementary measure. Wiberg/Mayer indices can
support bond-change arguments but are not bond energies.

## NBO

Use donor → acceptor interactions and `E(2)` as orbital-delocalization evidence.
Do not label `E(2)` as an interaction or hydrogen-bond energy. Report NBO version,
donor/acceptor labels, occupancies and the structural context.

## ESP and MPI

Map electrostatic potential on a declared electron-density surface, commonly a
fixed density isovalue. Record units, global color limits, extrema and area
distribution. MPI is an area average of absolute surface potential and is not
the dipole moment.

## Fukui functions

State vertical vs adiabatic definition, geometry policy, charge scheme and the
multiplicities of N, N+1 and N-1 states. Combine Fukui evidence with ESP,
frontier contributions, sterics and actual barriers before assigning a reaction
site.

## Spin density

Spin-density colors correspond to spin channels/sign convention, not orbital
phase. Report integrated/atomic spin populations when the localization claim is
important.

## IRI, IGMH, NCI/RDG and QTAIM

Interaction surfaces locate attractive, weak and repulsive regions. They do not
alone quantify strength. Combine geometry, integrated descriptors, QTAIM
critical-point properties, NBO/EDA/SAPT where appropriate and an interaction or
binding energy.

## ICSS/NICS

Magnetic shielding surfaces are distinct from ESP and electron density. Record
tensor component, probe plane/height, sign convention and the aromaticity claim
being tested.
